The One太一

Correct causal reasoning is not a knowledge problem — frontier LLMs know the textbook math. It is a computational-terminability problem: when the number of confounder configurations that must be marginalized (2k) exceeds the model's reasoning budget, even flagships fail. An explicit causal engine does not. The One moves the combinatorial part of causal computation out of the reasoning chain and onto a deterministic engine, attaching a machine-verifiable credential to every answer.

Scope of the claim (pinned): demonstrated for known structure + given parameters + high combinatorial complexity. Not a claim that "causal reasoning is solved." Where the engine has no advantage is stated openly below.

Finding 1 · The Combinatorial Cliff

Four model bases across three families (gpt-5.1, deepseek-v4-flash, claude) all collapse on exact back-door adjustment once 2k crosses ~16–32. The engine is flat at 1.000, millisecond-scale, at every k.

Finding 3 · Why a Cognitive Layer

At k≥5, gpt-5.1 returned 87 / 98 causal probabilities that were precise to 4 decimals, carried no warning, and were wrong (mean error 0.047, max 0.454) — averaging ~5,726 tokens spent per wrong-but-confident answer. In one case it returned P = 1.0179 — a literally impossible probability, delivered with full confidence.

Finding 4 · Where It Does NOT Help (stated openly)

The discipline of this project is that boundaries travel with claims.

  • Low-complexity natural language: when numbers are correctly stated in prose and k is small (1–2), gpt-5.1 marginalizes correctly on its own (k=1: 24/24 exact; k=2: MAE 10⁻⁴). The engine has no measurable advantage there. The edge appears only at high k with structured input.
  • Ecological validity — the advantage extends past exact CPTs: when the conditional-probability table is estimated from finite data (n=50/200/1000) rather than given exactly — engine and LLM receiving the same estimated table — the engine's high-k edge survives: tie at low k, engine wins at k≥4 (the LLM's reasoning error exceeds the shared, irreducible estimation floor). The engine doesn't make bad data good; it just adds no error of its own. Still assumes a known graph — learning the graph from data (causal discovery) is the last unproven frontier.
  • A retracted result: an earlier "engine wins on prose" finding was a two-canceling-bug artifact — caught and retracted after it had been committed and frozen (REJ-002). Recorded, not hidden.
  • A closed hypothesis: the Lo Shu magic-square structure was tested as a routing/attention/causal prior across an expert's three falsifiable designs — all passed their accuracy criteria but failed the irreplaceability test (any equivalent structure does the same). Direction formally closed by mutual agreement.
Every failure and retraction is public in 《美丽失败录》/ Beautiful Failures, append-only. A project that can falsify its own claims — even frozen ones — is what earns the word "verifiable."
